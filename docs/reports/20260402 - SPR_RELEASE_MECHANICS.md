## 1. US SPR DRAWDOWN RATE -- PHYSICAL MECHANICS

### Maximum Theoretical Rate
- **4.4 million barrels/day** for up to 90 days (design spec)
- After 90 days at max rate, drawdown rate DECLINES as caverns empty
- At **1 mb/d**, the reserve can sustain continuous release for ~1.5 years

### Actual 2026 Release Parameters
- **172 million barrels over 120 days = ~1.43 mb/d average**
- Structured as an EXCHANGE (not permanent sale): companies receive barrels now, must repay ~200 million barrels later (20% premium)
- Release began flowing **March 19, 2026**
- First tranche already delivered: **45.2 million barrels** as of late March

### Cavern-by-Cavern 2026 Allocation (confirmed)
| Site | Barrels Released | Capacity | Location |
|------|-----------------|----------|----------|
| Bayou Choctaw | 10.0M | 76.0M (6 caverns) | Louisiana |
| Bryan Mound | 15.7M | 247.1M (19 caverns) | Texas |
| West Hackberry | 19.5M | authorized capacity TBD | Louisiana |
| Big Hill | TBD (remainder) | 170.0M (14 caverns) | Texas |

### Are Releases Front-Loaded or Constant?
- Historical pattern: **front-loaded announcement effect, steady physical flow**
- The 2022 Biden release was announced at 1 mb/d for 180 days but actual weekly flows varied
- Physical constraint: pipeline transfer, port loading, and refinery scheduling create a natural smoothing effect
- The announcement itself provides immediate psychological price suppression before physical barrels arrive

### Physical Constraints on Drawdown
- Oil is displaced by pumping RAW WATER into caverns
- **Gas encroachment** from surrounding salt formations has penetrated caverns at ALL four sites (Bayou Choctaw, West Hackberry, Big Hill, Bryan Mound)
- Gas encroachment REDUCES effective drawdown rate
- Each cavern emptying makes it physically larger (water dissolves salt), creating long-term structural issues
- **$100M+ in repairs needed** due to structural damage from Biden-era rapid drawdowns (per Energy Secretary Wright, 2025)
- Rapid withdrawal causes cavern compaction, pressure drops, and rock stress increases

### Maximum Sustained IEA-Wide Flow Rate
- Historical IEA coordinated releases have **NEVER exceeded 2 mb/d**
- Largest single week during 2022 response: 8.4M barrels (~1.2 mb/d)
- 2022 six-month average: ~1.0 mb/d from US alone
- **Realistic combined global max: ~2 mb/d sustained over months**

## 2. SPR INVENTORY DEPLETION TIMELINE

### Starting Point (late Feb 2026)
- **415.4 million barrels** (58% of 714M authorized capacity)

### Drawdown Math
| Timeline | Barrels Released | Remaining | % of Capacity |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|---------------|
| Start (Feb 2026) | 0 | 415M | 58% |
| End of March | ~45M | ~370M | 52% |
| End of April | ~88M | ~327M | 46% |
| End of May | ~131M | ~284M | 40% |
| End of June | ~172M | ~243M | 34% |
| If extended at same rate | ~215M by end July | ~200M | 28% |

### Post-Release Level: 243 million barrels
- **Lowest since 1984**
- On a net-import basis: approximately **37 days of coverage**
- Congress already had legislated sales bringing SPR to 238M by 2028 -- this crisis accelerates that timeline by 2 years

### "Minimum Safe Level" -- What Counts as Critical?
- **IEA requirement**: 90 days of net petroleum imports (but US is net exporter as of 2024, so technically exempt)
- **Political floor**: ~300M barrels has historically been treated as the level below which bipartisan concern intensifies
- **Operational floor**: Below ~250M barrels, max drawdown rate drops below 4.4 mb/d due to insufficient cavern pressure
- **True emergency floor**: ~180-200M barrels -- at this level, the SPR cannot respond meaningfully to another crisis
- At 243M post-release, the US is essentially **one crisis away from an empty reserve**

## 5. WTI vs BRENT SUPPRESSION SPLIT

### The Core Problem: SPR Barrels Hit WTI, Not Brent
- US SPR barrels enter the **Gulf Coast market** (Louisiana/Texas) -- this is WTI-linked supply
- European strategic reserves (France gasoil, German crude, UK products) are **Brent-linked**
- The ~400M barrel release splits roughly:
  - **WTI-suppressing**: ~252M barrels (US 172M + some Canadian/other Western Hemisphere)
  - **Brent-suppressing**: ~114.5M barrels (European contributions ~26.8% of total)
  - **Neither WTI nor Brent directly**: ~33.5M barrels (Japan, Korea, Australia -- suppresses Asian benchmarks/Dubai)

### Brent-WTI Spread Blowout
- Spread widened to **~$18/barrel** during peak Hormuz disruption (Brent $118, WTI ~$100)
- This is the widest since the mid-2010s oil market dislocations
- **Reason**: SPR floods Gulf Coast with barrels (suppresses WTI), while Brent prices the GLOBAL supply disruption from Hormuz
- European reserves are too small (~114.5M barrels total) to meaningfully suppress Brent

### When Do Brent-Suppressing Releases Taper?
- European countries have the SMALLEST reserves and the shortest runways
- UK (39 days post-release), France (70 days), Germany (76 days)
- **By May-June 2026**: Most European strategic releases will be exhausted or significantly reduced
- After that, the only remaining releases suppressing global prices are US exports (WTI-linked, takes 4-6 weeks to reach Asia) and Japan
- **Brent is structurally less protected than WTI by strategic releases**

### Geographic Mismatch Problem
- 84% of Hormuz crude flows to **Asia**
- US SPR sits in Louisiana/Texas -- sailing time to East Asia: **4-6 weeks**
- By the time Atlantic basin reserves reach Pacific markets, the crisis either resolves or escalates beyond what reserves can address
- 3,200 tankers idle globally, 200+ stranded in the Gulf

## 6. MARKET PRICING OF SPR EXHAUSTION -- THE 2022 PRECEDENT

### 2022 Biden Release: The Template
- **180M barrels released** at 1 mb/d over 6 months (March-October 2022)
- Immediate announcement effect: Brent dropped 4.88% on day of announcement, WTI dropped 6.99%
- Treasury estimated: gasoline prices lowered by **$0.17-0.42/gallon** (point estimate: $0.38)
- SPR fell from ~568M to ~372M barrels (lowest in 40 years at the time)

### When Did the Market Start Front-Running Exhaustion?
- Oil peaked in June 2022 (~$122 Brent) despite ongoing SPR releases
- The market effectively **looked through the releases by month 3-4**
- Prices didn't sustainably decline until Q3 2022, and that was driven more by **demand destruction and recession fears** than SPR volumes
- By September-October 2022, with the release winding down, the market had already priced in the end
- Refill purchases began December 2022 at lower prices

### Pattern Recognition for 2026
- **Months 1-2 (March-April)**: Maximum price suppression effect (announcement + initial physical flow)
- **Month 3 (May)**: Market begins discounting effectiveness; smaller contributors running low
- **Month 4 (June)**: Front-running begins -- traders price in the ABSENCE of future releases
- **Month 5+ (July onwards)**: SPR release ends or is drastically reduced; price spike risk INCREASES
- The "SPR put" evaporates and the market reprices to reflect actual supply/demand without the strategic buffer

### 2026-Specific Amplifiers
- The 2026 disruption is **5x worse** than 2022 (16 mb/d missing from Hormuz vs 3 mb/d at risk from Russia)
- Combined IEA releases of 2 mb/d cover only **31% of the shortfall**
- At 14 mb/d net missing flows, 400M barrels covers only **28 days** even at theoretical 100% instantaneous release
- The market WILL front-run exhaustion faster than 2022 because the deficit is structurally unbridgeable

## 7. SPR CAVERN STATUS -- SITE BY SITE

### Bryan Mound (Brazoria County, Texas)
- **19 storage caverns**, 247.1M barrel authorized capacity
- **1.5 mb/d drawdown capacity** (largest single site)
- Gas encroachment confirmed -- gas has intruded from surrounding salt formations
- 2026 release: 15.7M barrels allocated from this site
- Connected to Texas pipeline infrastructure

### Big Hill (Jefferson County, Texas)
- **14 storage caverns**, 170.0M barrel authorized capacity
- Stores both sweet (<0.5% sulfur) and sour (>0.5-2.0% sulfur) crude
- Gas encroachment confirmed
- 10 diagnostic workovers completed in 2024
- FY2026 plan: 0 remediations, 8 workovers

### West Hackberry (Cameron Parish, Louisiana)
- Storage capacity details less publicly available
- Gas encroachment confirmed
- 2026 release: 19.5M barrels allocated
- Stores both sweet and sour streams
- 10 diagnostic workovers across Big Hill, West Hackberry, and Bayou Choctaw in 2024

### Bayou Choctaw (Iberville Parish, Louisiana)
- **6 storage caverns**, 76.0M barrel authorized capacity (smallest site)
- Gas encroachment confirmed
- 2026 release: 10.0M barrels allocated
- Updated crude assays available for quality assessment

### Cross-Cutting Issues
- **Structural damage**: Rapid drawdowns under Biden caused cavern compaction; Energy Secretary Wright confirmed $100M+ repairs needed
- **Gas encroachment**: Ongoing problem at ALL sites; gas from surrounding formations penetrates caverns, reducing effective storage and drawdown rates
- **Cavern enlargement**: Each emptying cycle dissolves more salt, making caverns larger and potentially less stable (each cavern designed for 5 emptying cycles)
- **Crude quality**: Commingled storage produces sweet and sour streams at each site; quality varies by cavern
- **Pipeline constraints**: Physical bottleneck between cavern extraction and refinery/port delivery limits instantaneous throughput

## 8. TRADING IMPLICATIONS SUMMARY

### Timeline of Vulnerability
1. **Now through April**: Maximum SPR suppression effect. WTI more suppressed than Brent.
2. **May-June**: European reserves thinning. Brent-suppressing releases taper. Spread could widen further.
3. **July**: US 120-day program ends. The single largest contributor (172M barrels, 42% of total) goes to zero.
4. **August+**: Only Japan maintaining meaningful releases. Most IEA members below 90-day requirement. Market fully prices in exhaustion.

### Key Levels to Watch
- **US SPR at 300M barrels**: Political alarm threshold
- **US SPR at 243M barrels** (post-release): Operational concern, max drawdown rate begins declining
- **UK at 39 days / Australia at 27 days**: These countries CANNOT participate in any further coordinated releases
- **Brent-WTI spread >$15**: Signals that SPR is suppressing WTI but NOT Brent

### The Structural Bull Case for Brent
- SPR releases are overwhelmingly WTI-suppressing (63% US-origin)
- European Brent-suppressing reserves are small and exhaust first
- Asian demand (84% of Hormuz flows) is geographically mismatched with Atlantic reserves
- The market will front-run SPR exhaustion by Month 3-4 of the release
- Post-July 2026, the strategic reserve backstop is effectively gone

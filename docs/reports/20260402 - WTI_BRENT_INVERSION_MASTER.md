## How Brent Actually Works (BFOET Complex)

### The Brent Benchmark Is No Longer North Sea Crude
The Brent field itself produces **ZERO barrels**. Fully decommissioned. The benchmark is now priced off six grades:

**Combined North Sea BFOET:** ~550,000 b/d (hit 20-year low of 350,000 b/d during June 2025 maintenance)
**WTI Midland flowing to Europe:** ~1,100,000 b/d

### WTI Midland Now IS the Brent Benchmark
- Added to BFOE basket in June 2023 (first non-North Sea grade ever)
- **Sets the Dated Brent price 50-60% of the time** (95% in May 2023, its first month)
- Argus Media titled their analysis: "WTI swallows Brent"
- The Brent-WTI spread has become primarily a **freight rate proxy** (cost of shipping WTI Midland from US Gulf Coast to Rotterdam)

### Settlement Mechanism
- ICE Brent is NOT physically delivered like WTI. It cash-settles against the ICE Brent Index
- The ICE Brent Index is derived from the Cash BFOE 25-day forward market (paper cargo trading)
- Only ~35 cargoes/month of 600-700k barrels trade in the Platts MOC assessment window
- The entire benchmark that prices 2/3 of global crude depends on this thin physical market

### Brent's Vulnerability
- Physical base is North Sea + US Gulf Coast exports -- both far from Hormuz, so Brent's deliverability is intact
- BUT Brent's PRICE absorbs global supply/demand sentiment, including the Hormuz crisis premium
- SPR releases (European strategic stocks are Brent-linked) are specifically suppressing Brent
- This explains why Brent is lagging WTI despite being the "global" benchmark

## How WTI Actually Works (NYMEX CL)

### Contract Specs
- **Physical delivery** at Cushing, Oklahoma
- Deliverable grade: "Domestic Sweet" (API 37-42, sulfur <= 0.42%)
- Three delivery facilities: Enterprise, Enbridge (Ozark), Plains
- Only ~1% of contracts go to physical delivery; 99% roll or cash-settle

### Cushing Infrastructure
- 94 million bbl total storage capacity (~78M working)
- Current inventory: ~27.5 million bbl (building, not draining)
- 6.5+ mb/d of total pipeline capacity in/out
- Pipeline utilization from Permian to Cushing running at only ~60% -- crude now bypasses Cushing, flowing directly to Gulf Coast

### WTI Is No Longer Landlocked
The US crude export ban was lifted December 2015. Since then:
- US crude exports grew from near-zero to **4.0-4.1 mb/d**
- Permian-to-Corpus Christi pipelines: **2.25 mb/d at 99% utilization**
- Enbridge Ingleside (EIEC): **1.5 mb/d** export capacity, largest US terminal, 3 VLCC berths
- South Texas Gateway: **800,000 b/d**
- LOOP (offshore Louisiana): VLCC-capable import/export facility

WTI is now a **waterborne barrel**. The price is set by global supply/demand, not Cushing storage constraints.

### WTI's Structural Advantage in This Crisis
- Cushing is landlocked in Oklahoma -- physically immune to naval blockades
- Connected to Permian Basin (6+ mb/d), Canadian oil sands (Keystone), Bakken, SCOOP/STACK
- All domestic/Canadian production, zero Hormuz exposure
- The "safety discount" that WTI carried for decades has flipped to a **"security premium"**

# PART 5: WHY WTI FLIPPED ABOVE BRENT

## The Five Structural Drivers

### 1. Asian Refiners Panic-Buying US Barrels
~60 million barrels of US crude purchased for April delivery by Asian buyers. Japan, South Korea, India, China all normally drink from the Gulf. That tap is shut. WTI-linked physical barrels are the marginal replacement.

### 2. Brent's Deliverable Pool Is Impaired (Indirectly)
Three of five Dubai benchmark grades are locked behind Hormuz. Dubai's deliverability collapse is bleeding into the Brent complex through the EFS spread. SPR releases are specifically suppressing Brent-linked grades.

### 3. WTI Is the World's "Safe Barrel"
Landlocked production, pipeline-fed delivery point, zero naval/geopolitical risk. The decades-old "landlocked discount" has become a "security premium."

### 4. WTI Midland Now IS the Brent Benchmark
With WTI Midland setting Dated Brent 50-60% of the time, the two benchmarks are converging by construction. The spread was already compressing to freight-rate levels. The crisis pushed WTI above because demand for the physical barrel (WTI at Gulf Coast) exceeds the demand for the benchmark assessment (Brent at Rotterdam).

### 5. US Export Infrastructure = Advantage
Unlike 2011-2015 (export ban, Cushing bottleneck), US crude now flows freely from VLCC terminals at Corpus Christi and Houston. The infrastructure that once caused WTI's discount is now its advantage.

# PART 10: ENGINEERING ASSESSMENT

## Grade Substitution Problem
Asian refineries spent billions on hydrocracking, coking, and desulfurization units **calibrated for medium-to-heavy sour crude** (Arab Heavy API ~28, Arab Medium API ~31, Iranian Heavy API ~31). They cannot simply switch to WTI (API ~41, sulfur ~0.3%) without:
- Reduced throughput (lighter crude = less volume through coker/hydrocracker)
- Wrong product slate (more gasoline/naphtha, less diesel/fuel oil)
- Lower margins (refinery optimization is lost)

**WTI can partially substitute for light sweet grades** (Murban-type diets). It CANNOT replace the heavy sour barrels without massive blending with Canadian WCS, Colombian Castilla, or Mexican Maya. This blending scramble bids up ALL crude grades.

## The Roll Drag on BRENTOIL
Per project records, BRENTOIL tracks June Brent futures with ~$6/month roll drag. At $109/bbl, that's ~5.5% annualized. In a rapidly appreciating market, this is acceptable. In a flat or declining market, it's a significant drag.

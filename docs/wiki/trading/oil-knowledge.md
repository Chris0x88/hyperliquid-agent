## The Edge: Petroleum Engineering

Chris is a petroleum engineer with deep domain expertise in upstream oil and gas. This is the trading edge -- not generic quant signals, not chart patterns. Every AI analysis must start from physical supply/demand fundamentals.

Generic AI/quant trading systems get killed by smart quant funds farming retail. The edge is the user's thesis and petroleum engineering knowledge applied through the AI's analytical capabilities.

## First-Principles Analysis Framework

The research framework must cover:

- **Upstream fundamentals:** Supply capacity, infrastructure status, production rates
- **Supply lag/lead times:** How long to bring production online, repair infrastructure
- **Forward curve structure:** Contango/backwardation signals and what they imply about market expectations
- **Contract specifics:** Which month, which benchmark, which delivery point
- **Location spreads:** Brent-WTI, transport diversions, regional premium/discount
- **Spare capacity analysis:** Who can ramp fast (Saudi spare is near zero), who cannot (Venezuela = years)
- **Reversal catalysts:** Peace deals, strait reopenings, SPR releases -- what could actually move supply

## Why No Backtesting for Oil

Past volatility data existed when massive spare capacity existed globally. Now there is no physical spare capacity, facilities are destroyed, and fundamentals are NOT reflected in historical data. Oil history is useful only for risk management (volatility ranges, worst-case scenarios), never for strategy development.

## Why LONG Only

Oil is in a structural supply deficit driven by infrastructure destruction that cannot be reversed by diplomacy. The thesis: destroyed infrastructure takes years to rebuild regardless of political outcomes. Backwardation is mispriced because deferred months assume resolution restores supply, but physical damage does not respond to signed agreements.

Active trading (scaling in/out around the core) is encouraged. Shorting oil is not.

## Macro Framework: Debt Monetization via War

The macro thesis follows a 1970s playbook:

- Policy architects want: higher rates, weaker dollar, lower bond prices
- This enables: buying back sovereign debt at a discount
- The mechanism: oil rises, inflation persists, rates stay high, dollar weakens, bonds drop
- Historical parallel: 1973 and 1979 oil crises -- both led to the US emerging stronger as energy producer of last resort

## War Duration Indicators

Real-money commitments reveal expected duration better than headlines:

- **Aviation:** Major carriers cancelling routes months out
- **Insurance:** Lloyd's conflict-zone designations, P&I club coverage decisions
- **Government backstops:** Creation of insurance backstops signals private market refusal
- **Infrastructure repair timelines:** LNG turbine lead times (years), refinery rebuilds (years)

When insurance companies CANCEL coverage rather than reprice it, the market is pricing a long conflict.

## Information Warfare Rules

Assume ALL news is fake or has an agenda. For every data point, ask:
- Why is this being shared?
- What is NOT being shared?
- Who benefits from this narrative?

Hierarchy of trust: satellite imagery > ship tracking (verify AIS not spoofed) > independent OSINT > bank research (useful but biased) > government statements (propaganda first) > media narratives (last).
